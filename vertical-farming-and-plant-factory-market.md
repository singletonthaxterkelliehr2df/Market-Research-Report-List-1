<p><p>Vertical farming refers to the practice of growing crops in vertically stacked layers, often in controlled environments such as skyscrapers or warehouses. The main aim of vertical farming is to produce high-quality crops with reduced dependency on land, water, and pesticides, while maximizing space utilization.</p><p>A plant factory, on the other hand, is an advanced version of vertical farming that utilizes artificial lighting, temperature controls, and other technologies to optimize plant growth. These facilities offer a controlled environment for growing crops, enabling year-round production and consistency in yield.</p><p>The vertical farming and plant factory market is witnessing significant growth due to various factors. Rising global population, increasing urbanization, and the need for sustainable food production are driving the demand for efficient farming solutions.
