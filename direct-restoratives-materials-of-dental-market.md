<p><p>Direct restorative dental materials refer to materials used to repair damaged or decayed teeth on the spot during a dental visit. The main types include amalgam, composite materials, and glass ionomers. Amalgam is a durable alloy of mercury with other metals. Composite materials are tooth-colored resins that bond to the tooth structure. Glass ionomers release fluoride to protect teeth. Other materials on the market include resin-modified glass ionomers and compomers, which are hybrids of composite materials and glass ionomers.</p></p>
