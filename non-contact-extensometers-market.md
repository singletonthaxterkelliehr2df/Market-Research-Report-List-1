<p><p>Non-contact extensometers are devices used to measure the deformation or strain of materials without physically contacting them. There are two main types of non-contact extensometers: laser and video extensometers. Laser extensometers use laser beams to measure displacement, while video extensometers use cameras and image analysis software to track changes in material dimensions. Both types offer high accuracy and precision in measuring strains in various materials, making them valuable tools in material testing and research.</p></p>
