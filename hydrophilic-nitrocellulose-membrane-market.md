<p><p>Hydrophilic nitrocellulose membranes are categorized based on their pore sizes: below 0.45 µm, 0.45 to 6 µm, and above 6 µm. Membranes with pore sizes below 0.45 µm are commonly used for applications requiring precise filtration, such as protein analysis and nucleic acid transfer. Those with pore sizes between 0.45 and 6 µm are ideal for general laboratory filtration tasks. Membranes with pore sizes above 6 µm are typically used for larger particle filtration, such as cell retention and clarification processes.</p></p>
