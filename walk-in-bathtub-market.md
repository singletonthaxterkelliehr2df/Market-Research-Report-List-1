<p><p>Walk-in bathtubs are categorized into two main types: embedded bathtubs and independent bathtubs. Embedded bathtubs are designed to fit into existing bathroom spaces and are typically installed against a wall. Independent bathtubs, on the other hand, are standalone units that do not require a wall for support. Both types of walk-in bathtubs offer safety features such as built-in seats, handrails, and non-slip floors, making bathing more accessible for individuals with limited mobility. These options cater to a growing market of individuals seeking accessible and safe bathing solutions.</p></p>
