<p><p>Low light imagers are specialized cameras designed to capture clear images in low light conditions. The two main types of low light imagers are CCD (charge-coupled device) and CMOS (complementary metal-oxide-semiconductor) imagers. CCD imagers are known for their high-quality image resolution and sensitivity, making them ideal for professional and scientific applications. On the other hand, CMOS imagers are more cost-effective and energy-efficient, making them popular for consumer electronics and security cameras. Both types of imagers have unique advantages and are widely used in different industries.</p></p>
