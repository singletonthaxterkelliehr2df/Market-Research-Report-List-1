<p><p>Transfection reagents are used to introduce nucleic acids such as DNA or RNA into cells for research purposes. There are two main types of transfection methods: biochemical and physical. Biochemical transfection involves the use of lipid-based or polymer-based reagents to deliver nucleic acids into cells. Physical transfection, on the other hand, involves physical methods such as electroporation or microinjection to introduce nucleic acids into cells. Both methods have their advantages and limitations, and researchers choose the appropriate method based on their specific needs and preferences.</p></p>
