<p><p>DC fuses are categorized into two main types based on voltage: Low Voltage (＜1000 V) and High Voltage (> 1000 V) markets. Low voltage DC fuses are widely used in residential, commercial, and industrial applications where the voltage does not exceed 1000 V. On the other hand, high voltage DC fuses are designed for applications requiring voltages greater than 1000 V, such as power distribution systems, renewable energy projects, and electric vehicles. Both types of fuses play a critical role in protecting electrical circuits from overloads and short circuits.</p></p>
