<p><strong>The Food Humectants Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Natural</li><li>Synthetic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Food humectants are used to retain moisture in food products, preventing them from drying out. There are two main types of humectants: natural and synthetic. Natural humectants such as honey, maple syrup, and glycerin are derived from plant sources and are preferred by consumers seeking clean label products. On the other hand, synthetic humectants like propylene glycol and sorbitol are chemically produced and provide a cost-effective alternative. Both natural and synthetic humectants play a crucial role in the food industry by maintaining the quality and shelf life of various products.</p></p>
