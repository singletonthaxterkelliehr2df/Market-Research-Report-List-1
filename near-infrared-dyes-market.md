<p><p>Near-infrared dyes are classified into two main types: water-soluble dyes and solvent-soluble dyes. Water-soluble dyes are easily dispersible in water and are commonly used in biological applications for imaging and detection purposes. On the other hand, solvent-soluble dyes are compatible with organic solvents and are often used in industrial applications such as plastics, textiles, and printing. Both types of dyes offer unique advantages and applications in various fields based on their solubility properties.</p></p>
